# JessTrading — Weekly Reels Mix

## Distribution per batch (3 reels per week)

| Reel | Category | Lever options | Length | Scenes |
|------|----------|---------------|--------|--------|
| 1 | Hot take / Market reaction | curiosity, contrast, identity | 16-24s | 2-3 |
| 2 | Educational quick | educational, authority | 24-32s | 3-4 |
| 3 | Avatar persuasion / Objection | aspiration, fear, social proof | 16-24s | 2-3 |

## Hard rules

- **Mascot always present** — JT (the candle) is in every scene of every reel.
- **On-screen text mandatory** — every scene must include verbatim text overlay so the reel works on autoplay (mute).
- **CTA only in last scene** — only the final scene can include a soft CTA ("Follow @jesstrading", "Link in bio"). Scenes 1 to N-1 must NOT mention price, "lifetime access", "link in bio".
- **Each scene = one Veo clip = exactly 8 seconds.** No exceptions (Veo 3.1 Fast only supports 4/6/8s; we standardize on 8s).
- **Dialogue per scene ≤ 16 words** (so it fits naturally in 8s of speech).
- **Vary tones across scenes within the same reel.** Don't use the same tone in all scenes — Director must pick at least 2 different tones from the mascot palette per reel.
- **Each reel must use a DIFFERENT primary lever** within a batch. Same constraint as carousels.

## Scene structure (what Director must produce per scene)

Each scene needs:
1. **Setting** — where the candle is (trading desk, kitchen, gym, coffee shop, dark room with monitor glow, beach, etc.)
2. **Mascot expression** — picked from `mascot.json → expressions` (neutral, happy, smug, panicked, confused)
3. **Tone id** — picked from `mascot.json → tones`
4. **Dialogue** — literal words the candle says, ≤16 words
5. **On-screen text** — verbatim caption to overlay in CapCut (separate from dialogue, can be a punchier summary)
6. **Animation hint** — motion direction for Veo: how the candle moves, camera framing, what else moves in the scene

## Visual variety principles

- Each reel's scenes must happen in DIFFERENT settings — don't put all 3 scenes at a trading desk.
- Backgrounds should be specific and contrasted: a coffee shop / a gym locker room / a kitchen / a dark room with a single screen / a park bench / an empty boardroom — not generic "office".
- Mix close-ups of the candle (face fills 60% of frame) with wider shots (candle in environment).
- The contrast between "serious financial character" and "casual everyday backgrounds" is the comedic engine — lean into it.

## Anti-clichés

Avoid the reels-equivalent of cliché taglines:
- "While you sleep" (unless the scene is literally about sleeping)
- "The future is automated"
- "Plug and play"
- "No coding required"
- Generic "Robot voice does finance" — JT has personality, not a flat AI delivery.

## Hashtags

5-8 hashtags per reel, varied per reel and per batch. Avoid reusing the same pool across reels.
